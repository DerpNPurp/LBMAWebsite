# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Non-tech-savvy parents, primarily mothers and fathers of children ages 4-13, living in Los Banos, CA. They are searching for a safe, trustworthy activity for their child. They are skeptical of sales pressure and marketing language. They make decisions based on gut trust and community reputation, not credentials alone. They are looking for an instructor who will know their child's name.

## Product Purpose

LBMAA's public website exists to build trust with local families and invite them to try a free class. Success is not a signup — it is a family that feels confident enough to walk through the door. Enrollment is the downstream result of earned trust, not pressure.

## Brand Personality

Earned. Warm. Genuine.

The brand should feel like a neighbor vouching for KJN Guerra — not a marketing department. Serious where it needs to be (safety, credentials, track record), human everywhere else. Parents should leave the page thinking "I want my kid to learn from this person," not "I should sign up before the deal expires."

## Anti-references

- **Chain dojo aesthetic** (Tiger Rock, ATA, many franchised schools): aggressive red/black, contract pressure, discount urgency, flashy belts-as-product energy.
- **Tough-guy MMA gym**: dark, intense, "no excuses" tone. Appropriate for adult fighters; wrong for a family school.
- **Corporate kids activity brand** (Kumon, Sylvan, national franchises): over-polished, stock-photo families, manufactured warmth, no personality.
- **Influencer instructor**: self-promotional tone, personal-brand focus, more about the instructor's image than what happens to the kids.

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth before credentials.** Lead with who KJN Guerra is as a person. Let credentials do quiet work in the background, not the foreground.
2. **Every word earns its place.** Copy must sound like a real person wrote it — someone who cares about kids and isn't trying to impress anyone. No marketing language, no em dashes, no filler phrases.
3. **Safety signals, not salesmanship.** Trust is built by showing what you do (photos of real kids, real certifications) — not by claiming it in adjectives.
4. **No pressure, ever.** CTAs invite. They never push. The tone throughout is "come see for yourself," not "act now."
5. **Light and open.** Physically: light backgrounds, generous space, no dark drama. Emotionally: approachable, not intimidating.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. The audience includes parents who may be on mobile, may have lower digital literacy, and should never feel lost or overwhelmed by the page.
